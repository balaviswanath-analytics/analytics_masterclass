Discipline 1

D1: Establish the Team

The objective is to ensure the right people are involved with clear roles and accountability from day one. Ambiguity in ownership at D1 compounds into missed deadlines and disputed findings at every subsequent stage.

Practical Tip

Keep the core team small — 3 to 5 people — for efficiency. Expand consultation as needed without expanding ownership. Too many owners means no accountability.

Required D1 Actions

Before any analysis begins, complete every item in this list:

  • ▶Assign Problem Owner (PSI) — one person accountable end-to-end
  • ▶Assign Lead Analyst — responsible for executing the analysis
  • ▶Assign Reviewer/Gatekeeper — independent validation role (must be separate from analysis team)
  • ▶Identify SMEs and stakeholders to be consulted at key stages
  • ▶Create RACI matrix if issue spans multiple teams
  • ▶Set up communication channels (Slack, email, meeting cadence)

Discipline 2

D2: Define the Problem with Precision

Objective: Ensure the team is solving the right problem, not a convenient one. The single biggest cause of wasted analytical effort is beginning analysis before the problem is precisely framed.

What Makes a Problem Statement "Well-Formed"

CharacteristicWhat This Means in Practice
SpecificUses precise metrics, not vague descriptions. "Dropped 23 percentage points" not "decreased significantly."
BoundedClear scope — what IS affected and what IS NOT affected. Boundaries prevent analysis sprawl.
Evidence-backedBased on data, not anecdotes or assumptions. Every claim must be verifiable.
Multi-perspectiveConsiders impact across customer, business, and operational dimensions simultaneously.
Time-boundStates precisely when the problem started or was first observed. The timestamp anchors Change Analysis later.
MeasurableIncludes a clear baseline and current state that can be quantified and tracked over time.

The Problem Statement Template

[METRIC] changed from [BASELINE VALUE] to [CURRENT VALUE] ([MAGNITUDE/DIRECTION]) for [AFFECTED SEGMENT] starting on [DATE/TIME] in [LOCATION/SYSTEM], impacting [CUSTOMER/BUSINESS IMPACT].

Example Problem Statements by Domain

Telecom • Customer Experience

Activation success rate dropped from 94% to 71% (23 percentage point decrease) for prepaid eSIM customers in Circle X starting January 15th after the network upgrade, impacting approximately 15,000 daily activation attempts and causing 3,450 additional customer care escalations.

E-Commerce • Journey Analytics

Checkout abandonment increased from 22% to 40% (+82% relative increase) for mobile Safari users aged 25–34 starting February 1st after the payment gateway upgrade, resulting in an estimated $250K daily revenue loss and 500+ customer complaints about payment failures.

Banking • Billing / Revenue

Auto-debit failure rate increased from 0.8% to 4.2% (5.25x increase) for credit card customers on recurring payments starting on the 15th of last month following the payment processor migration, affecting 12,000 customers and generating $180K in late fee reversals and collections costs.

SaaS • System Performance

API response time degraded from 250ms (p95) to 1,850ms (p95) for /user/profile endpoint starting at 14:30 UTC on Feb 5th, affecting premium tier customers in US-EAST region, causing 200+ support tickets and 15 customer escalations to the executive team.

Variation vs Standard Framework

Always articulate both what IS happening and what SHOULD be happening. This contrast is the analytical foundation of the KT IS/IS NOT work that comes next.

Standard (Expected Behavior)Variation (Actual Behavior)
Activation success ≥ 92%Activation success = 71%
Order processing time < 2 hoursOrder processing time = 6–8 hours
Bill accuracy ≥ 99.5%Bill accuracy = 96.8%
System uptime ≥ 99.9%System uptime = 97.2% (multiple outages)
Non-Violable Rule

A launch decision (Proceed / Redirect / Reject) must be documented BEFORE any deep analysis begins. This decision must be made by the Analytics Lead or Product Owner within 24 hours of issue identification and recorded in the Analysis Register.

Why Sequence Matters: The D2 Consequence

Skipping D2 results in solving the wrong problem and wasting all analytical effort that follows. Every hour invested in a vague or incorrect problem statement will cost 5× the hours downstream when you realize you've been analyzing the wrong phenomenon.

Exit Criteria for D1 & D2 (Non-Violable)

The Reviewer must approve ALL of the following before analysis proceeds to D3 or D4:

  • Problem Owner, Lead Analyst, and Reviewer are named and notified
  • Problem statement can be explained to a neutral third party without clarification
  • Quantitative evidence supports all claims in the problem statement
  • IS and IS NOT boundaries are clearly defined (sets up KT analysis in D4)
  • Baseline and current-state metrics are documented with sources
  • Communication channels and review cadence are established
  • Launch decision (Proceed / Redirect / Reject) is documented in Analysis Register
