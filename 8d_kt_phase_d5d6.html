Discipline 5

D5: Solution Design

A permanent solution must directly remove the validated root cause. Solutions that address symptoms rather than causes are the primary driver of repeat issues and recurring 8D investigations.

Design Gate Rule

The proposed solution must map directly to each validated root cause from D4. A solution that cannot explicitly explain how it removes the root cause of occurrence AND the root cause of escape must be redesigned before proceeding.

Solution Design Criteria

CriterionWhat This Means & Why It Matters
Removes Root CauseDirectly addresses the identified root cause, not just symptoms. This is non-negotiable — verify the causal chain explicitly.
No New RisksDoes not introduce new failure modes or customer impact. Every solution that changes system behavior carries new risk; this must be assessed and mitigated.
TestableCan be validated before production deployment. If you cannot define a test that would tell you whether the solution works, the solution is not well-designed.
ScalableWorks under expected load and growth scenarios. A fix that works at today's volume but fails at 2× volume is a ticking clock, not a solution.
MaintainableCan be supported by the operations team long-term without specialized knowledge or heroic effort.

Discipline 6

D6: Implementation & Validation

Staged rollout is not optional for P0/P1 issues. Rolling out to 100% before validating success at lower traffic volumes creates risk of deploying a fix that introduces a new failure.

Critical Pre-Implementation Step

Define success criteria BEFORE implementation begins. A team that starts measuring after deployment will rationalize any outcome. Success criteria must be locked in writing and reviewed by the Gatekeeper before the first deployment step.

Containment Removal Protocol

Only remove D3 containment actions after the permanent solution has been validated for a defined hold period (minimum 48 hours at full rollout for P0/P1, 7 days recommended). Document the removal as a formal step and monitor closely for 24 hours post-removal.

Exit Criteria for D5 & D6

Both D5 and D6 exit criteria must be met before progressing to D7:

  • Solution design documented and approved by Reviewer with explicit root cause mapping
  • Test plan created with clear pass/fail criteria defined BEFORE implementation
  • Pre-production testing completed (unit, integration, load, regression)
  • Solution implemented via staged rollout with validation gates between stages
  • Validation metrics meet success criteria for the defined hold period
  • Containment actions removed safely with no incident in 24-hour post-removal window
  • Final deployment documentation complete
